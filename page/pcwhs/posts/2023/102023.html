Inspiring Art-vironment Workshop for a Greener Tomorrow


YES-O's 'Art-vironment' Workshop was held during the Science Month 2023 celebration on October 2, 2023. It was a vibrant blend of artistic expression and environmental awareness where participants delved into critical environmental issues, fostering responsibility and activism through a poster-making contest with the theme Art-Vironment: Environmental Protection and Art Unite.

Renowned artist and PCWHS alumnus, Mr. Jestoni Rubantes, shared his artistry and contest-winning tips by igniting creativity. Joined by Mr. Joel Calalo of Guhit Pinas Young Artist Group, they guided the PCWHS learners in harnessing the power of art to bridge knowledge gaps and advocate for change.

As YES-O Adviser Mr. Romeo A. Darag rightly said, the workshop aligns perfectly with Science Month's theme, emphasizing the role of Science, Technology, and Innovation in a sustainable future.

PCWHS young talents from Junior High School and Senior High School across year levels participated enthusiastically. Winners of the poster-making contest will be revealed during the closing ceremony.

Honoring Our Teachers at PCWHS!


Pasay City West High School came together to express heartfelt gratitude to our remarkable educators on October 6, 2023, 1:00 p.m. at the school gymnasium.

The day kicked off with the Senior Scouts' patriotic flag presentation, followed by CIC President John Nathan Baterzal's heartfelt prayer, acknowledging our educators' unwavering dedication. Mr. Agapito Teodoro N. Manaog, our esteemed school principal, shared inspiring words, emphasizing the remarkable commitment of our teaching staff.

Faculty officers organized raffle draws with cash prizes generously provided by our school principal, sparking surprise and joy at the celebration. The PCWHS Dance Troupe captivated the audience with their spectacular intermission number, and games led by Naomi Isabel Galano and Jemrick Sabuero added a competitive spirit to the event.

The School Parent-Teacher Association (SPTA) recognized loyalty and retirees, while students expressed their gratitude with heartfelt Gifts of Gratitude to their beloved teachers. SSG President Kristel Jame Briosos concluded the event with a powerful reminder of the vital role our educators play in shaping the future.

A big round of applause to all who made the PCWHS Teachers’ Day celebration a memorable and heartwarming event! Happy Teacher's Day!

Safe After the Shake!


Pasay City West High School learners immediately went down to the school grounds when an earthquake hit NCR.

School administrators likewise left their posts and stayed on the school grounds. Teachers conducted headcounts for their respective classes to ensure that no one was left in the classroom. Mr. Agapito Teodoro N. Manaog, the school principal, immediately called the Pasay City School's Division Office for instructions regarding the classes. While waiting for instructions from SDO Pasay, members of the School Risk Reduction Management Council inspected the classrooms to check for cracks in the walls. At 9:00 a.m., as per the instructions from SDO Pasay, the school principal ordered the learners to return to their classrooms.

No Parking Policy Successfully Implemented at PCWHS School Grounds!


Effective Date: October 17, 2023

We are pleased to announce the successful implementation of the No Parking Policy within our school premises. This significant transformation, driven by the unwavering dedication of PCWHS Principal, Mr. Agapito Teodoro N. Manaog, represents a crucial step in ensuring the safety and security of our students, teachers, and all stakeholders while on school grounds.

Considering that unforeseen disasters, such as fires, earthquakes, and other emergencies, can occur at any moment, it is paramount that we take precautionary and anticipatory measures to safeguard everyone from potential harm.

Thanks to the exemplary leadership of Mr. Agapito Teodoro N. Manaog, this change has been seamlessly integrated to create a safer environment for all members of our school community.

We extend our heartfelt gratitude to our students, teachers, parents, and visitors for their cooperation in adhering to the No Parking Policy. Your unwavering support has played an instrumental role in enhancing the safety of our school community.

Together, let us continue our collaborative efforts to make our school a safer place for everyone.
